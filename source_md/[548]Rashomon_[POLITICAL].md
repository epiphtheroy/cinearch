Interpretations abound.

One might interpret the bandit's assault in *Rashomon* as a simple critique of feudal authoritarianism through Gramscian Hegemony. Or perhaps view the woodcutter's deception solely through the lens of 1950s Japanese post-war cynicism amid Allied occupation. 

Revelations emerge elsewhere.

However, the action's true ethical-political significance emerges elsewhere. **The Narrative Action** is the collective unreliability of testimonies that fractures objective truth and justice. **The Critical Lens** is Foucauldian regimes of truth, applied to the post-9/11 era of surveillance and misinformation. The core thesis posits that this narrative fracturing not only exposes ethical voids in individual accounts but critiques systemic power structures where truth becomes a tool of domination, mirroring contemporary political manipulations like those in the 2016 U.S. presidential election.
