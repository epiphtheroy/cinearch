Filming mirrored reality.

Rashomon's production began in 1950. 
- The diegetic gate, set in 12th-century Kyoto, embodies feudal anarchy with its crumbling structure amid relentless rain, symbolizing eroded social order.
- Extradiegetically, Kurosawa shot exteriors in Kyoto's war-scarred landscapes, where 1945 Allied bombings left 40% of the city in ruins, including damaged temples near the actual Rashomon site's historical footprint.
- This synergy arose from economic necessity: Japan's GDP had plummeted 50% by 1946, forcing Daiei Studios to use bombed-out areas for authenticity, inadvertently infusing the film's decay with the tangible despair of occupation-era poverty.
